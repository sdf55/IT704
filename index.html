After Care

Glycerin

Salt Water

To give the glass a glossy look, the may rub a tiny drop of glycerine (small bottles are sold in the cake decorating aisle of a grocery store) onto the glass.

Let the glass dry and it will have a glossy finish. True seaglass is porous, therefore it will absorb some of the glycerine and make it shiny.


Some people prefer the natural frosted look that the glass already has. If this is the case, simply washing the jewelry with warm water, possibly mixing in table salt will be sufficient.










About Us

Marian Guidoboni lives in New Hampshire, a mere half hour from the coastlines and the harbors of Portsmouth and Kittery. A mother of three, and a loving wife, Marian has always enjoyed the ocean, inspired by her husband’s personal interests in boating and the sea. She began creating sea glass jewelry out of pure interest, until she gathered enough support around her for it become not just a hobby, but a service as well.

All glass used in her creations are gathered from select island's, shorelines, and various other locations along the coastline of New Hampshire and Maine, and are only 100% authentic. The glass has not been altered in any way, shape, or form, other than the way it is found, resulting in a more natural rustic look.

Each piece is handcrafted by Marian herself, and personally sealed and delivered to your destination with care. Hours go into each pieces design, and even more into the actual creation of the jewelry items. One thing is always certain; at Made By Marians, each piece is unique in almost every way possible.
